## Design Context

### Product
Youform — a form, survey, and quiz builder positioning itself as the most affordable way to get advanced form-building features. Unlimited forms and responses on the free tier. Built with Jigsaw (static site) + Tailwind + Alpine.js + Blade.

### Users
Small and medium businesses shopping for a form tool. They want advanced features — logic, custom domains, file uploads, embeds, integrations — without Typeform-tier pricing. Context when they land: comparison-shopping, often fed up with what they're currently paying. They need to feel two things fast: "this is actually affordable" and "this looks good enough to put in front of my customers."

### Brand Personality
Three words: **confident, scrappy, warm.**

- **Confident** — not defensive about being cheap. The price advantage is the headline, not an apology.
- **Scrappy** — indie-maker energy, hand-built feel, not a bloated enterprise product.
- **Warm** — friendly, human, approachable. Not cold SaaS.

Emotional goals: **relief** (finally, an affordable option that isn't ugly) + **reassurance** that the forms the user builds will actually look good to their own customers.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined neo-brutalist playful.** Keep the existing DNA — hard black drop-shadows (offset, no blur), bold 2px black borders, warm cream backgrounds, saturated accent colors, subtle rotation, personality — but execute it with more precision. Tighter spacing rhythm, better typographic hierarchy, more intentional color ratios, fewer random elements.

**References (love):**
- **Gumroad** — neo-brutalism done with craft. Warm cream base, bold flat color accents, confident display type, hard shadows, no gradients, personality in copy, clear visual hierarchy.
- **Bannerbear** — similar warm cream + hard shadow energy, playful without feeling amateur.

**Anti-references:**
- Typeform (glossy, corporate, overdesigned)
- Generic SaaS dashboards (Linear clones, purple gradients, glassmorphism)
- AI-slop neo-brutalism (shadows on everything, same card repeated 6x, garish color chaos)

**Theme:** Light. Warm cream background (existing `medium-champagne` / `#F6E1A8` base) with tinted off-white surfaces. SMBs browse this during workdays on laptops; cream + saturated accents is what Gumroad/Bannerbear nailed.

### Typography
**Display: Bricolage Grotesque** (Google Fonts, free, variable)
Distinctive grotesque with subtle wonk and warmth. Variable axes for width, weight, and grade give real control over character at display sizes. Has personality without being precious. Pairs with the neo-brutalist hard-shadow aesthetic because it's confident without being sterile.

**Body: Hanken Grotesk** (Google Fonts, free, variable)
Warm, highly legible grotesque. Not on any of the overused reflex lists (not Inter, not DM Sans, not Plus Jakarta). Matches the "warm" brand word while staying clean enough for long-form reading at small sizes.

Scale: fluid `clamp()` for marketing-page headings, fixed `rem` steps for dense UI. Minimum 1.25 ratio between steps. Reserve all-caps for short labels only.

### Color
Keep the existing warm neo-brutalist palette but use it with discipline (60/30/10 visual weight, not confetti):

- **Base surface:** `medium-champagne` (#F6E1A8) — warm cream, 60% weight
- **Ink:** near-black tinted slightly warm, never pure #000
- **Primary accent (CTAs, emphasis):** `coral` (#FF7F4A) — 10% weight, keep it rare
- **Secondary accents (used sparingly for variation):** `aquamarine` (#45AD94), `pale-violet` (#C994FF), `lavender-rose` (#FF94E7), `vivid-yellow` (#FFE711), `sasquatch-socks` (#FF4F7E)
- **Neutrals:** tint subtly toward the champagne hue so surfaces feel cohesive with accents, not separate

Convert working colors to OKLCH when tuning tints/shades — it's perceptually uniform in a way HSL isn't. Reduce chroma as lightness approaches extremes.

### Design Principles

1. **Affordable ≠ cheap-looking.** Every surface should reassure an SMB owner that their own customers will take their forms seriously. Price confidence comes from design confidence.
2. **Rhythm, not repetition.** Neo-brutalism fails when every element has the same shadow, same border, same rotation. Vary spacing, vary which elements get the hard-shadow treatment, vary which get plain borders or no container at all. Tight groupings with generous breathing room between them.
3. **The 10% accent rule.** Coral is the hero color — use it for primary CTAs and moments of emphasis only. Other palette colors appear at most once per section for variation. Warm cream + ink does 80% of the work.
4. **Character over chrome.** Personality comes from typography choices, writing, unexpected layout decisions, purposeful rotation — not from piling on decorative effects. Remove anything that doesn't earn its place.
5. **Respect the gravity of real forms.** The product is serious (data collection, business workflows) even if the brand is playful. Never let the decoration interfere with clarity, legibility, or trust signals around pricing and features.

### Accessibility
Target WCAG AA by default. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — skip rotation/hover transforms for users who opt out. Never rely on color alone to convey state (pair with iconography or text). Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large display and UI elements. The hard-shadow-on-colored-button pattern is fine aesthetically but always verify text contrast on colored backgrounds.
